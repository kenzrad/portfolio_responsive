About Me

My name is Kensey, though my first name is actually Marion. I'm named after my Nana (my mother's mother), she was a first generation Scottish immigrant (Protestant) who married an Irish immigrant (Catholic). This, at that time, was a major problem for THEIR mums and dads (who disownded them), but it was not a problem for them (the young lovers)! They happily settled in Pennsylvannia, had my mum (among others) who later met my dad (also living in PA). Fast-forward to Germany a few years later, and they had me! Fast-forward 12 years, and we are in Virginia!

I have spent most of my life in Virginia. After a northern-virginian adolescense, I moved to Richmond to study Biology and Chemistry at VCU as an undergraduate. After working in academic and industry for a bit, I went back for my Master's at VCU. There, I studied oysters and molecular genomics (two of my favorite things). I have always loved SCIENCE and NATURE, so naturally - I married the two in my graduate research (and through in a flatworm for good measure).

During grad school, I got really into rock climbing. In the picutre above, you can see me climbing a boulder-problem in Nevada (Kraft Boulders, Plumber's Crack) a few years back. I also enjoy fly-fishing, stream monitoring (benthic macroinvertebrates, baby!), and playing with my sweet animals: Jelly, Beans, and Hemi.
